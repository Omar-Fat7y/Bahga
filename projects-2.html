Black Route

Our Route is black in black. Here where people took tombs as homes. Sultan, Amir, Commoner all under the same ground. The Northern Cemetery, Al-Qarafa, The city of the dead. All of these names belong to one place that includes great Islamic architectural elements, the monuments there are so amazing and authentic, they are worth to be visited such as madrasa, mausoleums, domes, sabil, tombs and complexes that gathering these Islamic architectural elements together. The cemetery contains about 36 monuments considered as the best Islamic monuments that reflected the beauty and the skillfulness of the Islamic architecture and included in the UNESCO heritage site.

Women`s Domes

We start our black tour with the domes that belong to the two wives of Sultan Al-Naser Mohamed, The Tomb and the remains of the Khanqah of Umm Anuk that dates back before 1348 AH / 749 AD, Bahari Mamluk Era. It’s regarded as the first women Khanqah that was constructed by the Sultan Al-Naser for his wife Khawand Tughay al-Nasiryah Umm Anok, She became from just a slave to the favorite wife of Sultan Al-Nasir as he gave her the title of Khawand” that means the first lady of the country, Al-Maqrizy said that Sultan Al-Naser didn’t love any woman as he loved Umm Anok. And probably the sister of prince Aqbogha Abd el-Wahed. Inside the remains of the khanqah there’s Qur’anic inscriptions part of the throne verse from Qur’an written on it inlaid in with blue, green and white mosaic tiles.
The dome of Tulbay that lies in front of the dome of umm Anuk. It dates back before 1364 AH / 765 AD, Bahari Mamluk Era. It was constructed by Bahari Mamlouk sultan An-Naser for his wife princess Tulbay bent Abdullah al-Nasirya. The Tomb contains the dome where the princess is buried that shows the skillfulness of the Mamlouk architecture. Inside the dome there’s remains of Qur’anic inscriptions. The existence of the 2 domes facing each other is giving them amazing view.

The Promise of Plum

Here where our story begins. This symmetrical Khanqah was constructed in 803 to 813 AH/1400 to 1410 AD, the era of the Burji Mamluks. It was constructed by Sultan Al Naser Faraj Ibn Barquq to fulfill his father Sultan Barquq's wish as he wanted to be buried in the northern cemetery near the Sufi Sheikhs’ tombs. Sultan Faraj Ibn Barquq ascended the throne when he was thirteen after his father's death. He decided to face the danger of the Mongol but he wasn't brave enough like his father. His reign ended by stabbing him to death. Sultan Barquq was the first Mamluk sultan to be buried in this cemetery beside Sheikh Al-Aref Belah Al-Hadramy. This building is very symmetrical building that was rare in the architecture of this period and was called the Twin Mosque. Sultan Faraj was the first one to build a complex in the Qarafa and Barquq was the first Mamluk Sultan be buried there.
The complex consists of a Khanqah for Sufis, two Sabils, two Kuttabs, two mausoleums, two domes, two minarets, and a congregational mosque which contains the stone pulpit of Qaitbay that imitates the wood and three prayer niches. The Khanqah contains a courtyard and colorful glass windows. In addition to two decorative mausoleums with domes. It was restored in modern times.

Domes Stop

In El Ashraf Street there is the complex of the great sultan Al Ashraf Barsbay and 3 wonderful domes, dome of Qurqumas which moved to here after it was attached to Al-Hakim Mosque, dome of Ganibek who was a symbol of loyalty to his Sultan and the dome of brother of sultan Al Ashraf Amir Yashbk Al Mahdy. Complex Al-Ashraf dates back to 835 AH/1433AD, reign of sultan Al Ashraf Barsbay, Burji Mamluk era. It was built by the panther king Barsbey who was mamluk of Sultan Barquq then became sultan in 1422. He was warrior and builder sultan and his greatest achievement was the conquest of Cyprus after 3 campaigns until it became under his control and he humiliated its King and put his crown in his complex at Al-Moez and the importance of the complex is from the importance of its owner who was buried in it.
The complex consists of mausoleum with a wonderful Dome that is covered with geometrical star patterns, Khanqah and two Sabils but the mosque was used as Madrassa. At the mosque, we can see rectangular area from the main entrance leads to the prayer hall with two iwans and mihrab from el Ghamri mosque and there is a door enters to the dome and there is the Khanqah with two iwans and two courts which contains dome of Amir Yashbk Al Mahdy.

Dome of "Don’t Know”

This dome belongs to sultan " Don't Know “, who was afraid to simply interact in the internal affairs of Egypt by answering every question with only one phrase “I don`t know” so the Egyptians called him sultan "Don't Know" or "Bakhsha" in Turkish language which later on was transferred to our common Arabic as "Ma'rafsh“. The dome dates back to 904 AH / 1498 AD, Burji Mamluk era. It was founded by sultan el-Zahir Qansuah Abu el-Sa’id who ruled Egypt for only one year and was famous for Sultan Ma’rafsh. Another interesting story about this specific dome tells us that it was called "The guardian dome" or 'Qubbet el-Ghafir’ in Arabic after the person responsible for the whole Qarafa who was the first inhabitant person to live in the cemetery & inside this specific dome, so whenever anyone needed this man, they found him here.
The dome is so beautiful of a triple arrow design and the rectangular pattern at the corners of the transition zone are unusual. The dome is also regarded a unique dome especially because it lies in the middle of the street surrounded by cars all day also there are lamps used at night to enlighten the dome and surrounded by a new sidewalk in order to avoid accidents or damage to the dome. It was added specific type of implement that doesn`t require water to preserve the dome.

The Master

Egypt decided to adorn the Egyptian pound by the magnificent complex of the one who succeeded in perpetuating his name. It dates back to 877-879 AH/1472-1474 AD, the reign of the "Master of Islamic architecture" Sultan Qaytbey, The Burji Mamluk Era. Sultan Sayf Al-Din Al-Ashraf Qaytbay indulged his love of architecture by having a funerary complex built for himself in addition to the 85 buildings around the Muslim world; 17 were built in Egypt alone. That’s why Sultan Qaytbay is considered to be "The Master of Islamic Architecture".
It is the largest complex in the whole area that still preserves many of its details and it helped in showing the original form of the complex while comparing to the others. The mosque has a unique decoration of mandala art inlaid with beautiful colors in the central skylight. The most unique feature in the mosque that there is a footprint attributed to the prophet. As for the elements of the Complex: (Bab Qaytbay-Maq'ad Qaytbay-Hawd Qaytbay- Rab' Qaytbey-Mausoleum and madrasa of Qaytbey-Sabil-kuttab Qaytbey). All the complex restored by Antiquities Preservation Committee and Khedive Abbas but the most important restorations made by ARCHiNOS that also started the cultural programme by organizing events in and around the Maq‘ad.

The Sleeping Beauty

Here is lying the Egyptian sleeping beauty on the pearl bier. The dome dates back to 1947 AD, Muhammad Ali dynasty. It belongs to a woman who searched for love her entire life but apparently she never found it, a woman with 5 short marriages, a woman who wasn’t beautiful but was one of the richest women at her time, a kind-hearted noble charitable woman who was loved by the Egyptians & a great supporter of Egyptian women, this is princess Shavikar of Turkish origins of Muhammad Ali dynasty. Her first husband was Prince "Ahmed Fouaad" who later on became the king of the kingdom of Egypt, they both gave birth to a baby boy "Isamiel" who died before completing one year and he's buried with her in the mausoleum.
This woman is buried here in a splendid way under a luxurious Italian marble bed of the belonging to the Italian architect "Gabarille Donatelli". The bed is beyond words and one can be amazed with its appearance, it costs about hundred thousand pounds gold at that time. It’s of a Mamluk architectural design having the four Iwans in addition to dome windows on which stained glass was painted stucco while the surface is painted with prominent pink decorations that appear a tablecloth with a pillow on it the emblem of the royal copper crown.

The Iconic Complexes

Once you enter El-Qarafa these complexes will catch your eyes immediately. First, The complex of Inal which dates back to 855 AH- 860 AH / 1451 AD -1456 AD, reign of Sultan Inal. Al-Ashraf Inal was one of Burji mamluks who was Illiterate and Parsimonious even on himself. He was known as Inal Al-Agrod because he doesn`t have a beard and the first one to bring the Julban Mamluks. It is the only complex that represents different ranks in the life of one sultan and even though it is built for a sultan but from glance the great anomaly in its construction is striking. The complex consists of Qubba (Mausoleum), mosque-madrasa, Sabil, minaret and Khanqah. The design of this complex is totally constructed anomaly and clearly appeared in constructing its components separately and then attached with a wall which gave him great irregularity in its facade installation.
Attaching to it the Complex of Qurqumas dating back to 911 AH-913 AH / 1505 AD -1507 AD, reign of Sultan Qansuh al-Ghuri, Burji Mamluk era. Qurqumas was the commander-in-chief of the armies. It the only surviving complex that still have a palace in a funeral complex in a good condition. It was restored by the Polish-Egyptian mission and they found more than 100 skeletons in the crypts of the complex.

Dome of Afandina

Our last but not least Afandina dome. The dome dates back to 1311AH/1894AD, Abass Helmi II, Muhammad Ali dynasty. The dome was founded by Princess Amina Elhamy in order to be bury her husband khedive Tawfiq that’s why the dome was called ‘Afandina’ after him. Amina Elhamy was a very beautiful woman & kindhearted charitable woman who was surnamed as "Um el-Muhssinen" meaning "Mother of Charity", she was also a prominent woman whose life started by her marriage till her son's accession to the throne as "Walida Pasha".
A more than 100 years tomb that still preserving all its vivid colours details as if it was built yesterday. We’re talking about the second most beautiful and largest mausoleum in the whole world after Taj Mahal in India. The mausoleum takes the cruciform mosque with four iwans The dome is having 7 members of the royal family including: Princess Amina Elhamy, Khedive Tawfiq, Muhammad Ali Tawfiq, Princess Fathiya, Princess Bamba Qadin (Um Abass), Muhammad Ali Abdel Meniem. And Khedive Abass Helmi II who continued the renaissance of his grandfather Mohamed Ali.
